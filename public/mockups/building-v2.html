The verdict, in plain English.

updated 14 Apr 2026
sources: HPD · DOB · DOF · RGB

Manhattan Plaza is one of the most storied rent-stabilized buildings in the city, and the numbers mostly back up its reputation. A 1977 HUD-financed complex originally built for middle-income tenants, it's now 100% rent-stabilized with Section 8 subsidies layered on — meaning tenants here enjoy some of the strongest protections available in New York.

The building has 12 open violations HPD-VI-OPEN ↗, which is fewer than the Manhattan median for buildings of this size. Response times are reasonable: violations tend to close in about 38 days, compared to 72 days borough-wide HPD-CLOSE-2024 ↗.

The weakest axis is the landlord relationship: tenants report slow responses on heat and elevator issues, and two harassment complaints were filed in the last 24 months. Both were resolved without a finding — worth knowing, but not damning.

▲ What this means
If you can get in, you stay. Turnover is low, protections are strong, and the rent ceiling is predictable. Expect slower-than-ideal maintenance response — bring patience, keep receipts.
